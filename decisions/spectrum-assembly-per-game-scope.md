# Decision: Spectrum Assembly Per-Game Scope (v1 first-draft)

## The decision

Per-game unit-count budgets for all 44 entries in the Spectrum Assembly track (per [spectrum-assembly-track.md](spectrum-assembly-track.md)). Total scope: **2,396 units across 44 entries**. First-draft estimates — single-pass with the inputs *tradition's canonical depth*, *techniques owned primarily vs inherited*, *art/audio budget*, *ship-bar requirements*. Numbers will firm up during build; treat as floors.

October 2026 launch unaffected — Shadowkeep Arcs 1-2 (32 units) is the only Assembly artefact in scope for October.

## Why

The brainstorm that produced [spectrum-assembly-track.md](spectrum-assembly-track.md) and [shadowkeep-four-arc-framing.md](shadowkeep-four-arc-framing.md) settled the *structure* of 44 entries but left *per-game scope* implicit. Without honest unit budgets, the long-tail of per-game spec drafts and the cut hierarchy both lack anchoring.

This decision provides those anchors. It also surfaces three findings:

1. **Three Tier 1 games are bigger than v0.2 implied.** V1.6 Embergate, V1.7 The Lantern Path, and V3.20 Edge of Iron all inherit primary teaching that was previously in Shadowkeep's Arcs 3-4 (combat, classes, XP, spells, dialogue, quest state, branching narrative). The redistribution lands them at 64-80 units each rather than the smaller "Tier 2 game scope" implied earlier.
2. **Some canon is genuinely small** — Jetpac (Voidlift) at 28, Tetris (Quoin) at 28, 3D Monster Maze (Long Passage) at 32. Inflating these to match other games would be dishonest to canon. Small games stay small.
3. **Total scope ~67% of platform direction-of-travel.** Memory's 3,584-units-per-platform was the direction-of-travel under the 16-games-per-platform framing. With 44 entries at honest per-game scope, we land at 2,396 — well under the platform target. This is healthy: leaves room for future canon additions and for per-game scope to grow during build.

## Heavy and light extremes

**Six games at ≥80 units (the heavy hitters):**

- Coldstar (96) — Elite tradition
- The Marchlands (88) — Next tilemap RPG
- Whitewinter (80) — Lords of Midnight tradition
- Embergate (80) — top-down RPG (post-redistribution)
- The Lantern Path (80) — Hobbit-class parser (post-redistribution)

Three of those (Embergate, Lantern Path, Marchlands) are big because of the Shadowkeep Arc 3-4 redistribution applied in [shadowkeep-four-arc-framing.md](shadowkeep-four-arc-framing.md). The other three (Coldstar, Whitewinter, Coreworks) are canonically large traditions.

**Eight productions at ≤32 units (the small / focused):**

- Hairline (20), Living Surfaces (24), Voidlift (28), Geometry's Pulse (28), Quoin (28), Towerfast (32), The Long Passage (32), The Long Night (32)

These are honest to canon — Jetpac WAS small; 3D Monster Maze WAS small; demoscene productions are focused. Inflating them would be dishonest.

## Tier hierarchy refined with scope

The track decision had Tier 1 = games 1-16, Tier 2 = games 17-32. With scope numbers:

- **Tier 1 (16 games)** = ~960 units — Spectrum Assembly's spine
- **Tier 2 (16 games + 4 late additions = 20 entries)** = ~880 units — the expansion + genre gaps
- **Tier 3 (V5 + V6 = 8 productions)** = ~380 units — demoscene + Next-native

Tier 1 is the right spine — most-load-bearing canonical traditions. Late additions (Greycoat, Drawn World, Quoin, Baize) fold naturally into Tier 2 alongside slots 17-32 by their thematic identity (they sit in V1-V4 by topic, but post-canonical-game-coverage by importance). V5 and V6 are correctly Tier 3.

**Possible Tier 3 demotion candidates** if canon doesn't hold:

- **#8 The Last Banner** (wargame, 48 units) — canon is thin; Lothlorien-tradition wargames are real but the lineage doesn't compare to Smith / Ultimate / Singleton / Crow. If timeline tightens, this is the first Tier 1 game to demote.

## Cut hierarchy (refined with scope)

If timeline tightens during the multi-decade build, cuts work backwards:

1. **V6 Next-native (276 units)** drops first — entirely post-everything
2. **V5 demoscene (104 units)** drops next — adjacent tradition
3. **V1-V4 late additions (196 units)** drop next; within them by inverse-canonical-weight:
   - First: 35 Quoin (28u; falling-block puzzle, canon-thin)
   - Then: 33 Greycoat (56u) or 36 Baize (48u; sport-sim, niche)
   - Last: 34 The Drawn World (64u; illustrated parser, real canon)
4. **Tier 2 within V1-V4 cuts inside-out**; possible order:
   - V4 ↑3D entries (29 Coldstar 96, 30 Coreworks 80, 32 Stillwatcher 52) — 3D-canon-late cuts before sport-sim
   - V3 ↑ smooth-scroll (23 Grinder, 24 March Iron — one-shot scroll variants)
5. **Tier 1 games 1-16 are the floor** — cut last; if cutting Tier 1 the curriculum loses canonical coverage. Last-out: 8 The Last Banner (canon-thin) if it earns the demote.

## What this decision is and isn't

**Is:** First-draft per-game unit estimates derived from canonical depth + technique ownership + art/audio budget + ship-bar requirements.

**Isn't:**
- Final budgets (estimates will firm up during per-game spec writing and as building proceeds)
- A schedule (the timeline above is a *rough order-of-magnitude*, not a commitment)
- A constraint on per-game design quality (the spine is commercial bar; scope serves quality, never the other way)

## Drift triggers

- **Treating these numbers as ceilings.** They're floors. A game's real budget grows when building reveals canonical depth not captured in this single-pass estimate.
- **Treating these numbers as schedule commitments.** The shipping timeline is illustrative of pace, not a milestone calendar. Real schedule is driven by content-quality bar, not unit count.
- **Cutting Tier 1 games to fit budget.** Tier 1 IS the curriculum; cuts come from Tier 3 (V5/V6) first, then Tier 2 (late additions and V4 3D-entries), then Tier 2 within volumes by inverse-canonical-weight. Tier 1 is the floor.
- **Inflating small games (Voidlift, Quoin, Long Passage) to "match" big games.** Canon dictates scope, not aesthetic uniformity. Jetpac was small; Quoin should be small.
- **Re-tiering during build to avoid the multi-decade horizon.** The project is multi-decade by design. If a shorter horizon is required, that's a project-scope decision, not a per-game-scope re-tier.
