# Phase 11: Fighting Games (Lessons 5889-6272)

**Total:** 384 lessons across 8 tiers (48 lessons per tier)

**Phase Goal:** Master one-on-one combat systems. Learn frame data, combo systems, special moves, blocking, and competitive fighting mechanics.

**Prerequisites:** Phases 1-10 complete (especially combat from Phase 2, animation from Phase 4)

**Phase Philosophy:**
- Precision = core of fighting games
- Frame-perfect timing
- Risk vs reward (attack vs block vs throw)
- Move properties (startup, active, recovery)
- 48 lessons per game for depth
- Complete fighting games with rosters

**Game Progression:**
- **Tier 1:** Street Fighter II - Definitive fighter, special moves, 8 characters
- **Tier 2:** Mortal Kombat - Digitized sprites, fatalities, gore
- **Tier 3:** International Karate+ - Three-fighter brawl, point system
- **Tier 4:** Way of the Exploding Fist - Precision martial arts, realistic
- **Tier 5:** Yie Ar Kung-Fu - Weapon fighters, variety, early fighter
- **Tier 6:** Karateka - Cinematic fighter, scrolling stages, princess rescue
- **Tier 7:** Barbarian - Gore fighter, sword combat, fatalities
- **Tier 8:** Budokan - Multi-discipline martial arts, synthesis

**What Students Master:**
- Frame data (startup, active frames, recovery)
- Hitboxes and hurtboxes (attack zones, vulnerable zones)
- Special move inputs (QCF, DP, charge moves)
- Combo systems (cancel windows, juggling)
- Blocking (high, low, chip damage)
- Throws and grabs (unblockable)
- AI opponents (difficulty levels, pattern recognition)
- Character balance (different speeds, powers, ranges)
- Health bars and round structure
- Finishing moves (fatalities, special victories)
- 2-player versus mode

## Tier 1: Street Fighter II (Lessons 5889-5936)

**Goal:** Build Street Fighter II with special moves and combos.

**Why this game:** THE definitive fighting game. Special moves. Combos. 8 World Warriors. Set all modern fighting game standards.

**Game Requirements:**
- 8 characters (Ryu, Ken, Chun-Li, Guile, Blanka, Zangief, Dhalsim, E. Honda)
- Special moves (Hadoken, Shoryuken, Sonic Boom, Spinning Bird Kick, etc.)
- Input system (QCF, DP, charge moves)
- Combo system (normal cancels into special)
- Blocking (high/low, chip damage)
- Throws (command throw, normal throw)
- Round system (best of 3 rounds)
- Health bars
- 4 boss characters (Balrog, Vega, Sagat, M. Bison)
- Unique stages per character
- 2-player versus mode

**Skills Introduced:**
- Special move inputs (quarter-circle forward, dragon punch, charge)
- Frame data (startup, active, recovery, advantage)
- Hitboxes and hurtboxes
- Combo system (canceling normals into specials)
- Blocking mechanics (high/low, chip damage)
- Throw system (command grabs vs normal throws)
- Character-specific move sets

## Tier 2: Mortal Kombat (Lessons 5937-5984)

**Goal:** Build Mortal Kombat with digitized sprites and fatalities.

**Why this game:** Digitized actors. Fatalities. Gore. Controversy. Different combo system (dial-a-combo). Huge cultural impact.

**Game Requirements:**
- 7 characters (Liu Kang, Kano, Sonya, Raiden, Sub-Zero, Scorpion, Johnny Cage)
- Digitized sprites (realistic appearance)
- Special moves (Spear, Ice Ball, Lightning, Fireball, Shadow Kick, etc.)
- Dial-a-combo system (input full sequence, then it plays out)
- Block button (dedicated block vs back-to-block)
- Fatalities (special finishing moves when opponent is defeated)
- Blood (LOTS of blood)
- Test Your Might mini-game
- Boss fights (Goro, Shang Tsung)
- Stages with hazards (pit, spikes)

**Skills Introduced:**
- Digitized sprite rendering
- Dial-a-combo system (vs SF cancel system)
- Block button (vs directional blocking)
- Fatality inputs and animations
- Gore system (blood spurts, decapitation, etc.)
- Stage hazards (knock into pit)

## Tier 3: International Karate+ (Lessons 5985-6032)

**Goal:** Build three-fighter simultaneous brawl with point system.

**Why this game:** Unique 3-fighter system. Point-based (not health bars). Fast-paced. Highly competitive.

**Game Requirements:**
- 3 fighters simultaneously (player vs 2 opponents)
- Point system (successful hits score points, first to 6 wins)
- Move variety (punches, kicks, sweeps, jump kicks)
- Deflections (perfect timing blocks attacker, scores point)
- Bonus stages (deflect flying objects)
- Multiple backgrounds (different countries)
- 2-player mode (vs each other plus CPU)
- Fast pace (quick rounds)

**Skills Introduced:**
- 3-fighter simultaneous combat
- Point scoring (vs health depletion)
- Deflection system (timing-based blocks)
- Bonus stage variety
- Fast-paced rounds

## Tier 4: Way of the Exploding Fist (Lessons 6033-6080)

**Goal:** Build precision martial arts fighter with realistic move set.

**Why this game:** Precision timing. Realistic martial arts moves. Belt progression. Early C64 fighter. Influential.

**Game Requirements:**
- 16 different moves (punches, kicks, sweeps, jump kicks, etc.)
- 2-player or vs CPU
- Belt progression (white to black belt)
- Knockout system (knock opponent down)
- Move interruption (hit during opponent's move startup)
- Backgrounds (dojo, temple)
- Referee (awards points)
- Timing-based gameplay (precise execution)

**Skills Introduced:**
- Move interruption (counter during startup)
- Belt progression system
- Referee and formal scoring
- Precision timing emphasis

## Tier 5: Yie Ar Kung-Fu (Lessons 6081-6128)

**Goal:** Build fighter with unique weapon-based opponents.

**Why this game:** Each opponent unique (weapons, styles). Boss rush structure. Variety.

**Game Requirements:**
- Player character (Oolong, martial artist)
- 11 unique opponents (each with different weapon/style)
  - Buchu (no weapon, fast kicks)
  - Star (throwing stars)
  - Nuncha (nunchaku)
  - Pole (bo staff)
  - Feedle (chain)
  - Chain (ball and chain)
  - Club (tonfa)
  - Fan (tessen)
  - Sword (katana)
  - Tonfun (dual tonfas)
  - Blues (armed, tough)
- Each opponent has unique attack patterns
- Health bars
- Single-player progression (beat all 11)
- Bonus point items (appear randomly)

**Skills Introduced:**
- Unique opponent variety (each fights differently)
- Weapon-based opponents
- Boss rush structure
- Adaptive AI (each opponent requires different strategy)

## Tier 6: Karateka (Lessons 6129-6176)

**Goal:** Build cinematic fighter with scrolling and story.

**Why this game:** Cinematic presentation. Scrolling fighter. Story (rescue princess). Beautiful animation. Jordan Mechner.

**Game Requirements:**
- Side-scrolling (walk right, fight enemies)
- Story (rescue Princess Mariko from Akuma)
- Enemy variety (guards, hawks, Akuma)
- Precise timing (high/low attacks and blocks)
- One-hit kills (if you get kicked while running)
- Bow animation (bow to princess or she kills you!)
- Checkpoints (restart from certain points)
- Cinematic cutscenes
- Death animations (detailed falls)

**Skills Introduced:**
- Scrolling fighter (vs static screen)
- Story integration (narrative in fighting game)
- Cinematic presentation (cutscenes, detailed animation)
- One-hit-kill mechanics (precision required)
- Running vs fighting stance switching

**Game Requirements:**

- Head chop (decapitation fatality)
- Kick to face (stun opponent)
- Neck bite (vampire wins)

- 2-player vs or vs CPU
- Multiple backgrounds
- Little goblin (drags away bodies)
- Health bars

**Skills Introduced:**
- Sword combat mechanics (slash, thrust, chop)
- Rolling dodge (invincibility frames)
- Decapitation (instant kill)
- Gore system (heads, blood)
- Unique mechanics (flying head, goblin)

## Phase 11 Outcome

Students have built **8 complete fighting games** and mastered:

**Core Combat Mechanics:**
- Frame data (startup, active, recovery, advantage)
- Hitboxes and hurtboxes (attack and vulnerable zones)
- Normal attacks (light, medium, heavy)
- Special moves (projectiles, anti-air, command grabs)
- Blocking (high, low, chip damage, block stun)
- Throws (normal, command, tech throws)

**Input Systems:**
- Motion inputs (QCF, QCB, DP, HCF, HCB, 360)
- Charge moves (hold direction, then opposite+button)
- Button combinations
- Input buffering (store recent inputs)
- Motion detection algorithms

**Combo Systems:**
- Cancel system (Street Fighter - cancel normals into specials)
- Dial-a-combo (Mortal Kombat - input full sequence)
- Link combos (frame-perfect timing)
- Juggling (hit airborne opponents)
- Combo damage scaling

**Advanced Mechanics:**
- Deflections and parries (International Karate+)
- Rolling dodge (Barbarian)
- Move interruption (Way of Exploding Fist)
- Style switching (Budokan)
- Weapon combat (Yie Ar Kung-Fu, Barbarian, Budokan)

**Game Structure:**
- Round system (best of 3)
- Health bars and time limits
- Point scoring (International Karate+)
- Knockouts and KOs
- Versus mode (2-player)
- Single-player progression (arcade mode, belt progression)
- Tournament structure

**Finishing Moves:**
- Fatalities (Mortal Kombat)
- Decapitation (Barbarian)
- Special victory animations
- Gore systems (blood, dismemberment)

**Character Design:**
- Character balance (speed, power, range trade-offs)
- Unique move sets per character
- Boss characters (stronger, unique abilities)
- Weapon-based characters
- Multiple fighting styles

**AI Systems:**
- Difficulty levels (easy to hard)
- Pattern-based AI (predictable but challenging)
- Adaptive AI (responds to player tactics)
- Unique opponent behavior (Yie Ar Kung-Fu)

**Presentation:**
- Stages and backgrounds
- Cinematic cutscenes (Karateka)
- Scrolling fighters (Karateka)
- Digitized sprites (Mortal Kombat)
- Gore and visual effects

**Ready for Phase 12:** Students transition from one-on-one combat to team-based sports with comprehensive sports games.
