# Phase 12: Sports Games (Lessons 6273-6656)

**Total:** 384 lessons across 8 tiers (48 lessons per tier)

**Phase Goal:** Master sports simulation and physics. Learn ball physics, team AI, player control, and authentic sports gameplay across multiple sports.

**Prerequisites:** Phases 1-11 complete (especially physics from Phase 10, AI from Phase 9)

**Phase Philosophy:**
- Realism vs fun (balance authenticity with playability)
- Ball physics critical (trajectory, spin, bounce)
- Team AI (positioning, plays, strategy)
- Player switching (control right player at right time)
- 48 lessons per sport for depth
- Complete sports games with seasons/tournaments

**Game Progression:**
- **Tier 1:** FIFA / Kick Off 2 - Soccer, team tactics, ball physics
- **Tier 2:** NBA Jam - Basketball, arcade over sim, dunks, fire
- **Tier 3:** Tony Hawk - Skateboarding, tricks, combo system, level exploration
- **Tier 4:** Sensible Soccer - Top-down soccer, fast-paced, aftertouch
- **Tier 5:** International Soccer - Early soccer, simple, elegant
- **Tier 6:** Summer Games - Track and field, button mashing, rhythm
- **Tier 7:** Winter Games - Skiing, skating, bobsled, variety
- **Tier 8:** World Games - International sports, variety, synthesis

**What Students Master:**
- Ball physics (gravity, bounce, spin, trajectory)
- Team AI (positioning, marking, plays)
- Player switching (auto vs manual)
- Sports-specific mechanics (dribbling, shooting, passing, tackling)
- Input systems (button mashing, rhythm, timing)
- Trick systems (combos, scoring)
- Physics simulation (momentum, friction, collisions)
- Tournament/season structure
- Multiplayer (2-4 players)
- Celebration animations
- Replay systems

## Tier 1: Kick Off 2 (Lessons 6273-6320)

**Goal:** Build soccer sim with advanced ball physics and team tactics.

**Why this game:** Best soccer game on C64. Revolutionary ball physics. Aftertouch. Team tactics. Fast-paced.

**Game Requirements:**
- 11 vs 11 soccer (full teams)
- Advanced ball physics (momentum, spin, bounce)
- Aftertouch (curve ball after kick)
- Team tactics (formations, styles)
- Player switching (auto and manual)
- Passing, shooting, tackling, heading
- Goalkeeper AI
- Throw-ins, corners, free kicks, penalties
- Tournament mode (World Cup, leagues)
- 2-player mode
- Replay system

**Skills Introduced:**
- Advanced ball physics (independent object, momentum)
- Aftertouch (apply spin after kicking)
- Team formations (4-4-2, 4-3-3, etc.)
- Player AI (positioning, marking, runs)
- Goalkeeper behavior (positioning, diving, catching)
- Set pieces (corners, free kicks)

### Lesson Structure

**Lessons 6273-6278: Ball Physics**
- L6273-6274: Ball as independent object (not tied to player)
- L6275-6276: Ball momentum and friction
- L6277-6278: Ball bounce and trajectory

**Lessons 6279-6284: Player Control**
- L6279-6280: Movement (8-way directional)
- L6281-6282: Dribbling (ball stays close to player)
- L6283-6284: Player switching (auto select nearest)

**Lessons 6285-6290: Kicking**
- L6285-6286: Passing (direction and power)
- L6287-6288: Shooting (aim and power)
- L6289-6290: Aftertouch (curve ball mid-flight)

**Lessons 6291-6296: Tackling & Defense**
- L6291-6292: Tackling (timing-based)
- L6293-6294: Slide tackle (risky, can foul)
- L6295-6296: Defensive positioning

**Lessons 6297-6302: Goalkeeper**
- L6297-6298: Goalkeeper AI (positioning)
- L6299-6300: Diving saves (direction + timing)
- L6301-6302: Catching vs punching

**Lessons 6303-6308: Set Pieces**
- L6303-6304: Corner kicks (aim and curve)
- L6305-6306: Free kicks (wall, curve, power)
- L6307-6308: Penalties (aim, power, keeper dive)

**Lessons 6309-6314: Team AI**
- L6309-6310: Formations (4-4-2, 4-3-3, 5-3-2, etc.)
- L6311-6312: Runs (teammates move into space)
- L6313-6314: Marking (defensive positioning)

**Lessons 6315-6320: Tournament & Polish**
- L6315-6316: Tournament structure (World Cup, leagues)
- L6317-6318: Replay system (save and view goals)
- L6319-6320: 2-player mode and final polish

## Tier 2: NBA Jam (Lessons 6321-6368)

**Goal:** Build arcade basketball with exaggerated dunks and "on fire" mechanic.

**Why this game:** Arcade over sim. "He's on fire!" Massive dunks. Fast-paced. Hilarious.

**Game Requirements:**
- 2-on-2 basketball (small teams, fast action)
- Exaggerated physics (high jumps, big dunks)
- Turbo button (sprint, jump higher, shoot harder)
- "On Fire" mechanic (3 baskets in a row = power mode)
- Ball trails and fire effects
- No fouls (arcade, not sim)
- Alley-oops (pass for teammate to dunk)
- Player roster (NBA players, unique stats)
- Tournament mode
- 2-4 player (4 total, 2v2)

**Skills Introduced:**
- Arcade physics (exaggerated, not realistic)
- Turbo button (sprint mechanic)
- Power-up state (on fire = boosted)
- Alley-oop coordination (teammate assist)
- No-foul arcade gameplay

## Tier 3: Tony Hawk (Lessons 6369-6416)

**Goal:** Build skateboarding game with trick combos and level exploration.

**Why this game:** Trick combos. Exploration. Collectibles. Objectives. Defined skateboarding games.

**Game Requirements:**
- Skateboarding physics (momentum, ollies, grinds, aerials)
- Trick system (kick flip, heel flip, 360 flip, etc.)
- Combo system (chain tricks for points, multiplier)
- Manual balancing (link combos on ground)
- Grind balancing (stay balanced on rail)
- Levels to explore (find lines, hidden areas)
- Objectives per level (collect S-K-A-T-E, high score, etc.)
- Special tricks (unlockable, high points)
- Create-a-skater
- Career mode (progress through levels)

**Skills Introduced:**
- Skateboard physics (momentum-based)
- Trick input system (direction + button combos)
- Combo multiplier (chain tricks without landing/bailing)
- Balancing mechanics (manual, grind)
- Exploration-based objectives
- Level design (skate lines, ramps, rails)

## Tier 4: Sensible Soccer (Lessons 6417-6464)

**Goal:** Build top-down soccer with fast-paced action and aftertouch.

**Why this game:** Top-down view. Incredibly fast. Accessible. Aftertouch. Massive following.

**Game Requirements:**
- Top-down camera (see full field)
- Tiny players (zoom out to see field)
- Fast-paced (quick matches, end-to-end action)
- Aftertouch (curve ball after kick, like Kick Off)
- Simple controls (one button for everything)
- Player switching (auto)
- Teams (hundreds of real teams, leagues worldwide)
- Tournament mode (World Cup, leagues, custom)
- 2-player mode
- Replays

**Skills Introduced:**
- Top-down sports view (vs isometric or side-view)
- Zoom level (tiny players, big field)
- One-button gameplay (contextual actions)
- Fast-paced sports (speed over complexity)

## Tier 6: Summer Games (Lessons 6513-6560)

**Goal:** Build track and field multi-event game.

**Why this game:** Olympic events. Button mashing. Rhythm timing. Event variety. Multiplayer party game.

**Game Requirements:**
- 8 events (100m sprint, long jump, javelin, 100m freestyle swim, skeet shooting, gymnastics, archery, 4x400m relay)
- Button mashing (rapid button presses for speed)
- Rhythm timing (press at right moment)
- Angle selection (javelin, long jump)
- 1-8 players (take turns, track scores)
- Opening ceremony
- Medal ceremony (gold, silver, bronze)
- World records (save best scores)

**Skills Introduced:**
- Button mashing mechanics (rapid input)
- Rhythm timing (press at optimal moment)
- Angle selection (trajectory aiming)
- Multi-event structure
- Turn-based multiplayer (8 players)
- Ceremony animations

- Photo finish

**Long Jump:**
- Button mashing for speed

- Angle selection for jump

**Javelin:**
- Button mashing for run-up
- Angle selection

**100m Freestyle:**
- Button mashing for swimming
- Rhythm for turns
- Stamina management

**Skeet Shooting:**

- 25 targets

**Gymnastics:**
- Rhythm inputs (press at right time)

**Archery:**

**4x400m Relay:**
- Button mashing

## Tier 8: World Games (Lessons 6609-6656)

**Goal:** Build international sports collection. Synthesis of Phase 12.

**Why this game:** Variety. Unique international sports. Synthesis of all sports mechanics learned.

**Game Requirements:**
- 8 international events (caber toss, log rolling, cliff diving, bull riding, sumo wrestling, barrel jumping, slalom canoeing, bike racing)
- Unique mechanics per event
- 1-8 players
- World records
- Cultural presentation

**Skills Introduced:**
- International sports variety
- Unique mechanics (caber toss physics, sumo balance, etc.)
- Everything from Phase 12 synthesized

### Event Breakdown

**Caber Toss (Scotland):**
- Power meter
- Angle selection
- Rotation (caber must flip end-over-end)
- Distance and landing orientation scored

**Log Rolling (Canada):**
- Balance mechanic (stay on rolling log)
- Speed control (make log roll faster)
- Last one standing wins
- 2-player competitive

**Cliff Diving (Mexico):**
- Dive angle
- Rotation tricks
- Entry (splash vs clean)
- Points for difficulty and execution

**Bull Riding (USA):**
- Balance (bull bucks, stay on)
- Timer (8 seconds)
- React to bull movements
- Points for time + style

**Sumo Wrestling (Japan):**
- Push opponent
- Balance (stay in ring)
- Timing (push when opponent off-balance)
- Ring-out or knock-down wins

**Barrel Jumping (Netherlands):**
- Speed (button mashing)
- Jump timing
- Distance (clear barrels)
- Landing (don't fall)

**Slalom Canoeing (France):**
- Paddle rhythm (alternate sides)
- Navigate gates
- Time + penalty for missed gates

**Bike Racing (Italy):**
- Pedaling (button mashing)
- Steering
- Sprint finish
- Stamina management

## Phase 12 Outcome

Students have built **8 complete sports games** and mastered:

**Ball Physics:**
- Independent ball object (not tied to player)
- Momentum and friction
- Bounce physics
- Trajectory calculations
- Spin and curve (aftertouch)
- Air resistance

**Player Control:**
- Movement (8-way, analog feel on digital)
- Dribbling (keep ball close)
- Player switching (auto and manual)
- Turbo/sprint mechanics
- Contextual actions (one button, different results)

**Team AI:**
- Formations (4-4-2, 2-3-2, etc.)
- Positioning (offense and defense)
- Marking (stay with opponent)
- Runs (move into space)
- Passing AI (choose best target)

**Sport-Specific Mechanics:**
- Soccer: passing, shooting, tackling, heading, set pieces
- Basketball: shooting, dunks, alley-oops, turbo
- Skateboarding: tricks, combos, manuals, grinds
- Track & field: button mashing, rhythm, angles
- Winter sports: skiing, skating, sledding
- International sports: unique mechanics per sport

**Input Systems:**
- Button mashing (speed generation)
- Rhythm timing (press at optimal moment)
- Power meters (hold and release)
- Angle selection (trajectory aiming)
- Balance mechanics (maintain equilibrium)
- Combo inputs (trick sequences)

**Arcade vs Simulation:**
- Realistic physics (Kick Off 2)
- Arcade physics (NBA Jam - exaggerated)
- Balance (fun vs authentic)
- Accessibility vs depth

**Game Modes:**
- Exhibition (single match)
- Tournament (World Cup, Olympics)
- Season/league (multiple matches)
- Career mode (progression)
- Multiplayer (2-8 players, turn-based or simultaneous)

**Presentation:**
- Opening ceremonies
- Medal ceremonies
- Celebration animations
- Replay systems
- Score tracking
- World records
- Player customization

**Physics Simulation:**
- Momentum (conservation, transfer)
- Friction (surface types)
- Gravity (jumps, dives, projectiles)
- Collisions (players, ball, environment)
- Air resistance
- Spin effects

**Ready for Phase 13:** Students transition from genre-specific games to multi-genre synthesis with large-scale projects combining all learned skills.
