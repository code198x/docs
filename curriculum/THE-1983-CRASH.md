# The Video Game Crash of 1983

**Purpose:** Document the North American video game crash - its causes, effects, and how it shaped everything that followed.

**Last Updated:** 2026-01-18

---

## Why The Crash Matters

The 1983 crash:
1. **Killed the first console generation** (in North America)
2. **Enabled Nintendo's dominance** - NES filled the vacuum
3. **Changed industry structure** - Quality control emerged
4. **Shifted gaming west→east** - Japan became gaming centre
5. **UK/Europe unaffected** - Different market dynamics

Understanding the crash explains why the industry looks the way it does.

## Part 2: The Catalysts

### E.T. The Extra-Terrestrial (1982)

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Developer** | Howard Scott Warshaw (solo) |
| **Development time** | 5 weeks |
| **Reason** | Christmas deadline |
| **License cost** | $20-25 million (to Spielberg) |
| **Units produced** | ~5 million |
| **Units sold** | ~1.5 million |
| **Units returned** | Millions |

**The legend:** Unsold cartridges buried in New Mexico landfill. (Confirmed in 2014 excavation.)

**Why it failed:**
- Impossible timeline
- Confusing gameplay
- Pit-falling frustration
- Didn't match movie quality

### Pac-Man (2600, 1982)

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Developer** | Tod Frye (solo) |
| **Development time** | ~5 weeks |
| **Units produced** | 12 million (more than 2600s sold!) |
| **Units sold** | 7 million |
| **Quality** | Poor - flickering, wrong maze |

Atari expected every 2600 owner to buy Pac-Man - a fatal assumption.

### Underlying Problems

| Problem | Impact |
|---------|--------|
| **Overproduction** | Inventory glut |
| **Quality collapse** | Consumer distrust |
| **Market saturation** | Too many consoles |
| **Price wars** | Race to bottom |
| **Retailer pain** | Returns, markdowns |

| Date | Event |

| Metric | 1982 | 1985 | Change |
|--------|------|------|--------|
| **US game revenue** | $3.2 billion | $100 million | -97% |
| **Atari workforce** | 10,000+ | ~1,000 | -90% |
| **Game publishers** | Dozens | Handful | -80% |

### Who Died

| Company | Fate |
|---------|------|

| **Magnavox** | Exited gaming |
| **Coleco** | Collapsed |

### Who Survived

| Company | How |
|---------|-----|
| **Activision** | Barely, pivoted |

| **Japanese companies** | Different market |

## Part 4: Causes Analysis

### Immediate Causes

| Cause | Mechanism |
|-------|-----------|
| **Overproduction** | Supply >> demand |
| **Quality collapse** | Shovelware ruined trust |
| **E.T./Pac-Man** | High-profile failures |
| **Retailer backlash** | Stopped stocking games |

### Structural Causes

| Cause | Mechanism |
|-------|-----------|
| **No quality control** | Anyone could publish |
| **Platform age** | 2600 limited |
| **Computer competition** | C64, etc. offered more |
| **Market immaturity** | No proven business model |

### What Atari Did Wrong

| Mistake | Consequence |
|---------|-------------|
| **Activision lawsuit** | Lost, legitimised third parties |
| **No quality control** | Market flooded |
| **E.T. timeline** | Flagship disaster |
| **Pac-Man assumptions** | Massive overproduction |
| **Ignoring warnings** | Believed own hype |

## Part 5: The UK/European Exception

### Why Europe Didn't Crash

| Factor | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| **Computer dominance** | Spectrum, C64, not consoles |
| **Different market** | Less saturated |
| **Bedroom coding** | Constant fresh content |
| **Budget games** | Affordable software |
| **Later console adoption** | Missed the 2600 boom |

### The UK Market (1983-1985)

| Aspect | Status |
|--------|--------|
| **Spectrum** | Booming |
| **C64** | Growing |
| **BBC Micro** | Schools, enthusiasts |
| **Consoles** | Minor presence |
| **Publishers** | Thriving |

While America crashed, UK gaming flourished.

### Implications

| Effect | Result |
|--------|--------|
| **UK game industry** | Developed independently |
| **Different aesthetics** | European style |
| **Computer culture** | Not console culture |
| **Later NES adoption** | 1987+ in Europe |

## Part 6: The Recovery

### Nintendo's Approach

| Strategy | Implementation |
|----------|----------------|
| **Quality control** | Nintendo Seal of Quality |
| **Lockout chip** | 10NES prevented unlicensed |
| **Limited third parties** | 5 games/year per publisher |
| **Marketing** | "Entertainment System" not "video game" |
| **Pack-in game** | Super Mario Bros. |

### The NES Launch

| Phase | Date | Details |
|-------|------|---------|
| **Japan** | July 1983 | Famicom launch |
| **US Test** | October 1985 | New York only |
| **US Rollout** | 1986-87 | Gradual expansion |
| **Europe** | 1986-87 | Later, mixed success |

### Why NES Succeeded

| Factor | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| **Quality games** | Super Mario Bros. |
| **Fresh start** | New generation |
| **Retailer confidence** | Nintendo took risk |
| **Toy positioning** | Different section in stores |
| **R.O.B.** | Trojan horse - looked like toy |

R.O.B. (Robotic Operating Buddy) existed to convince retailers NES was a toy, not a game console.

## Part 7: Industry Changes

### What Changed

| Before Crash | After Crash |
|--------------|-------------|
| Open platform | Controlled platforms |
| Anyone publishes | Licensed publishers |
| No QA | Quality requirements |
| US dominated | Japan dominated |
| Hardware focus | Software focus |
| Boom mentality | Sustainable growth |

### The Nintendo Model

| Element | Effect |
|---------|--------|
| **Licensing** | Publisher pays for privilege |
| **Manufacturing** | Nintendo makes cartridges |
| **Approval** | Nintendo reviews games |
| **Limits** | Prevents flooding |

This model persisted through SNES, was challenged by PlayStation.

### Long-Term Legacy

| Effect | Duration |
|--------|----------|
| **Platform holder control** | Continues today |
| **Quality expectations** | Permanent |
| **Japan dominance** | ~1985-2000s |
| **Console generations** | Industry rhythm |

## Part 10: Myths vs Reality

### Myth: E.T. Caused The Crash

**Reality:** E.T. was symptom, not cause. Structural problems existed.

### Myth: Games Died

**Reality:** Only in North America. Japan fine. UK/Europe booming.

### Myth: Crash Was Sudden

**Reality:** Built over years. 1983 was when dam broke.

### Myth: Quality Was Fine

**Reality:** Market flooded with terrible games. Quality did collapse.

### The Landfill

**Myth:** All unsold games buried.
**Reality:** Confirmed in 2014 - thousands of cartridges excavated.
