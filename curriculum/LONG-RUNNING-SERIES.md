# Long-Running Series: Franchises That Defined Eras

**Purpose:** Document game series that spanned multiple entries, platforms, and years - their evolution, consistency, and cultural impact.

**Last Updated:** 2026-01-18

---

## Why Series Matter

Long-running series show:
1. **Evolution of design** - How ideas developed over time
2. **Platform adaptation** - Same IP, different hardware
3. **Developer consistency** - House styles and quality bars
4. **Cultural touchstones** - Characters that transcended games
5. **Business models** - How sequels were marketed and sold

## Part 7: What Made Series Successful

### Consistency Factors

| Factor | Example |
|--------|---------|
| **Recognisable character** | Mario, Sonic, Dizzy |
| **Core mechanic** | Mario's jump, Mega Man's weapons |
| **Iterative improvement** | Each entry better |
| **Platform evolution** | Grew with hardware |
| **Quality bar** | Nintendo polish |

### Why Some Series Died

| Reason | Examples |
|--------|----------|
| **Creator left** | Matthew Smith, Rare post-Stampers |
| **Market changed** | 8-bit series in 16-bit era |
| **Quality decline** | Sonic modern era |
| **Publisher issues** | Dizzy post-Codemasters sale |
| **Franchise fatigue** | Annual releases |
